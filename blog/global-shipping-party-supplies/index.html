Global Shipping Guide for Wholesale Party Supplies

2026-06-05 · Logistics · Party Maker

Sea Freight vs Air Freight

For wholesale party supplies, sea freight is the standard choice. A 20ft container (33 CBM) can hold thousands of decoration sets, making per-unit shipping costs negligible. Typical transit times: 25-35 days to Middle East, 30-40 days to Europe, 15-25 days to Southeast Asia. Air freight makes sense for samples, urgent restocking, or high-value custom items where speed justifies the premium.

Understanding Incoterms

The most common incoterms for party supplies are FOB (Free On Board) and CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight). FOB means the supplier delivers to the port and you handle shipping from there. CIF includes shipping and insurance to your destination port. New importers often prefer CIF for simplicity, while experienced buyers use FOB to control their own freight forwarding relationships.

Customs Documentation Checklist

Essential documents for customs clearance include commercial invoice (showing product description, HS codes, declared value), packing list (carton count, weights, dimensions), bill of lading or airway bill, certificate of origin (if claiming preferential tariff rates), and any required safety certificates for your destination country.

Working with Freight Forwarders

A good freight forwarder is your logistics partner. They handle booking, documentation, customs clearance, and final delivery. Choose a forwarder with experience in party supplies — they'll understand the product characteristics (light but bulky) and optimize accordingly. Get quotes from 3-4 forwarders before each shipment to ensure competitive pricing.

Insurance and Risk Management

Marine cargo insurance costs approximately 0.2-0.5% of cargo value and protects against loss, damage, and theft during transit. Given the relatively low value of party supplies, many buyers skip insurance — but if you're shipping a full container worth $20,000+, the $40-100 premium is well worth the peace of mind.
