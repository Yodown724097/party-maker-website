How to Import Party Supplies from China: A Complete Guide

2026-05-20 · Buying Guide · Party Maker

  • Step 2: Find Reliable Suppliers
  • Step 3: Request Samples and Compare

Step 1: Research Your Market

Before importing, understand your local market demand. Which party themes are popular? What price points work? Are there seasonal peaks (Ramadan, Christmas, Halloween)? Research competitors and identify gaps in the market.

Step 2: Find Reliable Suppliers

Quality suppliers can be found through trade platforms (Alibaba, Made-in-China), trade fairs (Yiwu Fair, Canton Fair), or direct factory visits. Look for suppliers with export experience, positive reviews, and willingness to provide samples. A supplier who communicates clearly and responds promptly is worth their weight in gold.

Step 3: Request Samples and Compare

Always request samples from multiple suppliers before placing a bulk order. Compare material quality, printing clarity, packaging, and overall finish. Pay for samples — it's a small investment that prevents costly mistakes. Test products in real conditions (e.g., do paper plates hold food? Do decorations resist humidity?).

Step 4: Negotiate Terms

Key negotiation points include unit price, MOQ, payment terms (typically 30% deposit, 70% before shipping), delivery timeline, and shipping responsibility (FOB vs CIF). Build long-term relationships — loyal buyers get better prices and priority service.

Step 5: Arrange Shipping and Customs

Work with an experienced freight forwarder who handles party supplies. They'll manage documentation (commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading), customs clearance, and delivery to your warehouse. Consider sea freight for cost efficiency on orders over 1 CBM. For urgent restocking, air freight or express is faster but more expensive.
